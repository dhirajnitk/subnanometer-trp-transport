experimental_proposal.txt — Testable predictions of voltage-gated Trp switching
================================================================================

PREDICTION 1: Voltage-dependent Trp fluorescence in NaV1.4
----------------------------------------------------------
If the model is correct, the Trp fluorescence spectrum or lifetime of
NaV1.4 should change measurably as the membrane potential is stepped
from -80 mV to +50 mV.

Experiment:
1. Express human NaV1.4 (with beta1 subunit) in HEK293T cells
2. Patch-clamp in whole-cell configuration to control membrane potential
3. Excite Trp with 280 nm pulsed laser (<50 fs, <1 uJ/cm^2 to avoid damage)
4. Collect Trp emission at 327 nm with streak camera (<200 fs resolution)
5. Step V_m from -80 mV to +50 mV in 10 mV increments
6. Measure fluorescence intensity and lifetime at each voltage

Predicted signature:
- At -70 mV: Trp-57 dominates → fluorescence lifetime ~X ns, emission peak at 327 nm
- At +40 mV: Trp-156 dominates → fluorescence lifetime ~Y ns (different local environment)
- The fluorescence change should follow a Boltzmann sigmoid centered near -20 mV
  (midpoint of the occupation switch curve)

Control:
- Mutate Trp-57 to Phe: should eliminate the voltage-dependent fluorescence shift
- Mutate Trp-156 to Phe: should eliminate the shifted component
- Use tetrodotoxin (TTX) to block ion current without affecting field

PREDICTION 2: Voltage-gated FRET between Trp and a bound probe
---------------------------------------------------------------
If the exciton moves between Trp residues with voltage, then FRET from
a donor Trp to a nearby acceptor fluorophore should also be voltage-dependent.

Experiment:
1. Label NaV1.4 at a specific extracellular site with a fluorescent acceptor
   (e.g., Alexa-350 whose absorption overlaps Trp emission at 327 nm)
2. Measure Trp-to-Alexa FRET efficiency as a function of V_m
3. The FRET efficiency should change as the exciton population shifts
   between Trp residues at different distances from the label

PREDICTION 3: Anesthetic disruption of voltage-gated switching
--------------------------------------------------------------
General anesthetics (propofol, isoflurane) bind to hydrophobic pockets
in ion channels adjacent to Trp networks.

Experiment:
1. Repeat Prediction 1 with 10 uM propofol
2. Propofol binding should alter the local dielectric environment
   around specific Trp residues, changing their site energies
3. Predicted: the voltage midpoint of the fluorescence switch shifts
   or the switch amplitude diminishes

If all three predictions hold, voltage-gated Trp exciton routing is
real — and every action potential in every neuron is accompanied by
a wavefront of exciton population shifting through the protein's Trp
network, potentially gating ion channels at sub-picosecond timing.
